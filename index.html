About Me

Physicist. AI Enthusiast. Foodie. Runner.

I am a 6th year Ph.D. Candidate in physics at Yale University, with a focus on applying machine learning to improve detector performance and optimize signal sensitivity at the ATLAS experiment. I recieved my BA in both physics and mathematics from the University of Chicago.

Outside of physics and ML, I am deeply committed to improving gender equity in physics and STEM at large. I served as the chair (co-chair)for 3 years for both Women in Physics+ and Equity in the Job Search Symposium at Yale.

ATLAS Experiment at CERN

ML Applications to Particle Physics

For the past 6 years I have been a member of the ATLAS collaboration at CERN and conducted research for my thesis. The main analysis I have contributed to is a search for a rare Higgs boson decay to tau leptons. I have also worked on ML improvements for the Tau Trigger and I spent a year working on a Higgs boson to $ZZ_{dark}$ search, which combined the buzz wordy topics of ML, Higgs boson, and dark matter!

ML for Systems at Netflix

ML Research Intern, Summer 2021

I was lucky enough to be a part of Netflix's first ever summer intern class where I spent 12 weeks working with the Algorithms Engineering and Compute Team. I studied the feasibility of using time series predictions to model CPU requests to their cluster. This could help make their cluster run smoother and cheaper, while reducing waittimes for employees and users.

=Mu2e Experiment at Fermilab

Research at the Intensity Frontier

During my second year at Yale, I conducted researh Mu2e which is a planned Intensity Frontier Experiment at Fermilab. It is trying to detect the charged lepton flavor violating decay (which would be a beyond the standard model process) of a muon decaying directly to an electron. I worked on calorimeter triggers for moth signal electrons and high energy photons.
